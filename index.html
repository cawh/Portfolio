Moment

What if you could wear your schedule on your wrist? Moment re-imagines the way we keep time, and what we do with it.

Life is becoming busier. Keeping on top of our daily schedules is tough; Moment seeks to remedy this by integrating your schedule into your watch. The LED ring around its outer dial conveys different events over the next 12 hours, split into three coloured categories: social, work and personal. This aims to provide a visual cue as to what those events might be, as well as letting you view how you've spent this time (through the app) at a later date.

Two bodies for the watch were designed: one with lugs and one without. The lugless version is an excersive in simplicity; whilst the lugs give seek to give it's counterpart a more masculine, robust form.

Light from the LED ring is emitted from behind a series of small, machined holes around the dial of the watch. Subtly machined recesses around the crown and dial assist with grip.

Iris

Iris integrates a single iris diaphragm into the head of a desk lamp, allowing for direct control of the type of light exiting it's parabola.

Form finding through quick, dirty sketching. Choosing a desk lamp for the basis of this project allowed for the operation of the iris diaphragm to be experienced in a more meaningful, intimate way.

After developing a compact iris diaphragm that could be laser cut from acrylic and polypropelene, several lighting experiments were set up. Later a looks like, works like model was developed (although acknowledgedly not to the highest fidelity).
